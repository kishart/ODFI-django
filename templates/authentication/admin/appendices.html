This Internal Use section is strictly for ODFI workforce only. It contains the APPENDICES that accompany the standard operating procedures (SOPs) that all ODFI workforce must comply. This is so as to gain the confidence of our donors to keep giving, and hence our mission will be achieved, biiznillah. A copy of all the procedures presented here must be made available in hardcopy in all Municipal Islamic Centers (MICs) as well as at the ODFI Headquarters. All ODFI workforce must abide by the procedures given. BBM/ODFI will perform regular audits on the execution of the procedures given here from time to time. Results of the audits will be presented to our donors on a regular basis. The SOPs that accompany the appendices presented here are given separately in this website.

Appendices

APPENDIX 1 - SYLLABUS FOR NEW MUSLIMS

All new Muslims should master basic knowledge of Islam. All asatiz must make sure the reverts are educated in these topics appropriately. In addition, all ODFI members of workforce must master these basic topics too. We categorise basic Islam knowledge into 5 levels – Level 1, Level 2, Level 3, Level 4, and Level 5. Reverts should master Level 1 first, before going to Level 2, Level 3 and so on.


Level 1

Goal: Start practicing immediately with minimal confusion.

  • Who is Allah? — Tawheed (Oneness of Allah), names and attributes (basic level).
  • Shahadah — Meaning and implications.
  • Introduction to Solat (Prayer) — Importance of 5 daily prayers, and when they are.
  • Wudu’ (Ablution) — Step-by-step with practice.
  • Cleanliness in Islam — Basic hygiene, ghusl (major purification), and istinja.
  • Short Surahs — Start memorizing al-Fatihah and Surah Ikhlas.


Level 2: Strengthening Daily Practice
Goal: Establish regular worship and deepen faith.
  • Full Solat — Including physical positions and words (with meanings).
  • Aurat & Clothing — For both men and women.
  • Pillars of Islam — All five pillars in brief.
  • Pillars of Iman (Faith) — Basic belief in Allah, angels, prophets, books, Day of Judgment, Qadr.
  • Introduction to the Qur’an — What it is, its structure, and how to start reading.
  • Simple Du’as — Before sleep, eating, entering toilet, etc.


Level 3: Building a Strong Foundation
Goal: Strengthen belief and deepen ritual understanding.
  • Tawheed in more detail — Uluhiyyah, Rububiyyah, Asma wa Sifat.
  • Understanding Shirk — What nullifies tawheed.
  • Introduction to Prophet Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم — His character and why he is the final prophet.
  • Importance of Sunnah — Hadith basics, why Sunnah is followed.
  • Fasting in Ramadan — Rules and purpose.
  • Zakat — Basic concept (not yet full fiqh).
  • More Surahs — E.g., Surah Falaq, Nas, Kawthar.


Level 4: Living Islam
Goal: Integrate Islam into daily life and begin grasping fiqh
  • Fiqh of Worship — More detailed rulings on wudu, ghusl, tayammum, menstruation (for women).
  • Halal and Haram — In food, money, and daily dealings.
  • Friday Prayer and Jumu’ah Etiquette
  • Islamic Manners (Akhlaq) — With parents, neighbors, Muslims and non-Muslims.
  • Marriage in Islam — Roles, rights, and responsibilities.
  • Importance of Knowledge — Seeking ilm, reading Qur’an with tafsir.
  • Basic Seerah — Life story of Prophet Muhammad


Level 5: Growth & Commitment

Goal: Deepen love for Allah, refine practice, and prepare to be self-guided.
  • Intro to Fiqh Schools — Understanding differences respectfully.
  • The Role of the Ummah — Community service, dawah.
  • More Qur’an Memorization & Tafsir
  • Learning Arabic Language
  • Tazkiyah (Spiritual Development) — Purifying the soul, sincerity (ikhlas), gratitude.
  • Introduction to Islamic History
  • Dealing with doubts — Q&A on common misconceptions.



APPENDIX 1A ROLES OF MIC, BCC, MASJID, MUSOLLA

MIC (Municipal Islamic Center)

  • In general, the MIC is the ODFI administrative center for the whole municipality.
  • The MIC serves as the Islamic hub of the community, where advice on the Islamic way of marriage, divorce, birth, death, humanitarian aid, education, etc, are referred to.
  • Typically, there is/will be one MIC in every municipality.
  • Every MIC has several BCCs that the MIC is in charge of.
  • Every MIC must have 3 office bearers: one Person-In-Charge (PIC), one Secretary
  • (SMIC), and one Treasurer (TMIC). The 3 office bearers of the MIC (PIC, SMIC, and
  • TMIC) cannot be the same person (ie. the same person holding more than one
  • The MIC is headed by a PIC, who reports to the Regional Director (RD).
  • Every MIC should identify and train at least 3 local people, who already have means of livelihood, to become imams
  • In Cebu island, we aim to have 3 RDs (RD of Northern Region, RD of Central Region, and RD of Southern Region)
  • In Camotes (where there are 4 municipalities) there is 1 RD
  • In Bantayan (where there are 3 municipalities) there is 1 RD


BCC (Barangay Community Center)

  • The BCC is a satellite of the MIC.
  • A BCC should be opened in a community having at least 20 Muslims.
  • A BCC is housed in a building that is rented or bought.
  • The following factors must be taken into account when choosing the BCC premise:
  • accessibility, total floor area, total land area, possibility of complaints or disturbances from neighbours, (eg smell, noise, pigs, bad neighbour) cleanliness of surrounding, water and electrical supply, condition of the house and facilities available,
  • The location of the BCC must be at least 2 km from the nearest MIC and that the journey to the MIC is difficult due to lack of public transport, poor road conditions, etc.
  • The BCC premise is for religious and social activities only, and by right, not for accommodation.
  • The BCC must have at least 3 local imams who are the caretaker of the BCC.
  • The imams should already be living in the community not far from where the BCC is located.
  • If a BCC premise cannot be secured, look into the possibility of using the imam’s house, where no rent is required.
  • An operation budget of not more than 20,000 peso/month may be granted to the BCC.
  • The operation budget is NOT for paying the imam. Rather, it is to be used for rental or to buy refreshments whenever there are classes held there, or to pay for utility bills, or other job-related expenses of the BCC.
  • The BCC imams do not get any salary as this is a voluntary position that they do just to attain Allah’s pleasure. The imams maintain their regular salaried job or whatever livelihood means they already have, and perform imam duties outside of their salaried/ current livelihood working hours.
  • All BCC imams are encouraged to upgrade their knowledge of Islam continuously, and if they show potential, may be sent for more Islamic education that may lead to them becoming asatiz.
  • Wherever and whenever suitable the BCC should eventually be replaced by a masjid. The MIC must plan for this.
  • If there are 2 BCCs in one barangay, one of the BCC will be called SCC (Sitio Community Center).
    • MASJID
    Masjids (mosques) play a central and multifaceted role in the lives of Muslims and within the broader community.
    • The primary function of the masjid is as a place for performing the 5 daily prayers, Jumaat prayers, and special prayers such as Taraweeh in Ramadan and Eid prayers.
    • It is also the spiritual center of the community as it offers a quiet and sacred space for reflection, supplication (du‘a), recitation of the Qur’an, and remembrance of Allah.
    • The masjid is also an education hub, where Islamic studies are carried out for children and adults. Scholars and teachers often give lectures, especially after prayers or during special events. There can also be workshops in marriage, parenting, funeral rites, etc
    • The masjid is a platform where social activities that brings people together are carried out, promoting unity and brother/sisterhood. Community issues discussions, counseling, and conflict mediation are also carried in in masjids.
    • Masjids collect and distribute charitable donations (zakat and sadaqa) to the poor and needy. It provides community support like food drives, aid for the sick or bereaved, and support for disaster-struck community members.
    • Of course, the masjid is also a daawah center, where information about Islam for non-Muslims is available. It welcomes visitors regardless of tribe, race and religion.
    • During the last 10 days of Ramadan, the masjid becomes a spiritual retreat, where Muslims may stay in for i‘tikaf (dedicated worship, reflection, and detachment from worldly affairs).

    In short, masjids are spiritual centers as well as the foundations of Islamic civilization—places of enlightenment, service, and communal strength.


    MUSOLLA
    The musolla plays the role of the masjid in places where the masjid is too far to reach. However, the scope is smaller as its resources are smaller than that of the masjid.

    All new recruits of ODFI affiliated Centers must go through an orientation program led by the Du’at Supervisor (DS), the PIC, and other members of the workforce appointed by BBM and/or ODFI.



    Below are the points to be explained to and discussed with the new recruit/s. Most of the materials are contained in this SOP.


    1. Introduction to BBM & leadership of BBM (with regard to the Visayas)
    2. Some history of BBM in the Visayas, formation of ODFI & leadership of ODFI
    3. Friends of BBM in the Visayas (Cebu Care, Ummah Care, etc)
    4. Vision of ODFI
    5. Mission of ODFI
    6. Islamic Centers under ODFI in the Visayas
    7. ODFI Financial Principles
    8. ODFI Operational Principles
    9. ODFI Human Resource Principles
    10. ODFI Training & Development Principles
    11. Basic overview of this SOP and the requirement to comply to it
    12. The requirement of mastering basic Islamic knowledge
    13. Examination
    14. Pledge


    New Recruits PLEDGE (to be read loudly by new recruits in front of the RD, DS, AS, PIC and at least one other member of the workforce requested by the RD or DS)



    I pledge that when I prepare reports, to the best of my ability, the reports will be true and reflect the actual situation on the ground. To the best of my ability, all monthly OPEX budgets, daawah budgets and other budgets will be spent according to their intended purposes. I am aware that in order for my ibadah to be accepted and blessed by Allah SWT, integrity in planning, execution and accounting is critical. I know that all reports are forms of shahadah, which will be witnessed by Allah SWT- Al Bašir. The shahadah I mean here is not referring to the kalimat shahadah recited by reverts, but it is shahadah in the forms of reporting, and bearing witness in the presence of Allah that the reports are true, and the accounting is true.


    I am aware that the sources of the funds dispensed by BBM are predominantly zakat money given for the sake of Allah, with the intention that it will be used for fisabilillah, and that the zakat donors have entrusted BBM, ODFI and all MICs to faithfully honor this trust. Unless explicit prior permission has been given, the funds or the rice meant for daawah cannot be diverted for other use, including allocations which has never been budgeted for. I promise that any excess of funds or rice meant for daawah will be recorded and carried forward, and not spent or allocated for other uses. I understand that if I don’t do that, the money would not be halal. Any unauthorized allocation not done according to the intention of the original donors and their trustees would not be considered halal because it is a misuse of funds.


    I understand that huge funds are collected for specific purposes and targets. If the targets, for whatever reasons cannot be met, then the unused or surplus funds or rice stock cannot be spent for other purposes. It must be accounted and the balance carried forward to the following month or next daawah event.


    As such, I am fully aware that UNLESS money is spent for the purpose it was intended, and that the reports I write reflect actual situations on the ground, then there can be a risk of bearing false witness (false shahadat). I know that the ultimate accounting will be done by Allah SWT on the Day of Judgement, and I hope all my endeavors will be accepted and blessed by Him.


    Ya Allah, please forgive me, accept my deeds, and give me strength to perform all the tasks expected of me to the best of my ability.

    APPENDIX 17 - GUIDE TO DAKWAH

    1. Purify Your Intention

    • Your goal should be to please Allah and convey His message, not to win arguments or gain followers.
    • Remember: "Your duty is only to convey the message clearly." (Qur'an 16:82)

    2. Understand Your Audience

    • New Muslims: Focus on reminding them of Allah, encouraging practice, and correcting misunderstandings with gentleness.
    • Non-Muslims: Focus on Tawheed (Oneness of God), the purpose of life, and the beauty of Islam.
    • Respect their background, traditions, and level of understanding.

    3. Equip Yourself with Knowledge

    • Continuously increase your knowledge of Islam: syahadah, salah, fasting, zakat, hajj, and key beliefs (e.g., tawheed, prophethood, hereafter).
    • Learn how to answer common questions/misconceptions, e.g., about women in Islam, jihad, etc.
    • Speak truthfully and respectfully. If you don’t know the answer, say so and follow up later.

    4. Use Wisdom and Good Manners

    • Be kind, patient, and respectful even when facing hostility.
    • Follow the Qur’anic principle:
      “Invite to the way of your Lord with wisdom and beautiful preaching…” (Qur'an 16:125)

    5. Start with Common Ground

    • Build rapport: talk about shared values like kindness, family, honesty, and caring for others.
    • Use stories and examples from the life of the Prophet ﷺ — his honesty, mercy, and justice.

    6. Offer Materials

    • If available, provide translated Qur’ans, simple pamphlets, or daawah booklets.
    • Share useful websites or apps for those who want to explore further.

    7. Be Consistent and Available

    • Follow up with genuine care.
    • Help them build a connection with other Muslims and nearby MICs and musolla/mosques.
    • Support them through the SDC — don’t leave them alone.

    8. Make Dua

    • Always ask Allah to guide the hearts of those you're speaking to and to accept your efforts.

    APPENDIX 19 - FIQH OF QURBAN

    Fiqh of Slaughtering Animals for Eidul Adha (Qurban) – Quick Guide


      1. Who Must Offer Qurban?
      • Sunnah muakkadah (highly recommended) for those who:
        • Are Muslim
        • Are mukallaf (sane, adult)
        • Can afford it (after basic needs)
      • In some mazhabs (like Hanafi), it's wajib if financially able.

      2. Time of Slaughter
      • Starts: After Eid prayer on 10th Dhul Hijjah
      • Ends: Before sunset on 13th Dhul Hijjah
      • ✅ Can be done on 10th, 11th, 12th, or 13th (4 days total)
      • ❌ Slaughter before Eid prayer is invalid for qurban (though valid as normal meat)

      3. Animals Allowed
      • 🐑 Sheep/goat (1 person per animal)
      • 🐄 Cow/buffalo (can be shared by up to 7 people)
      • The animal must be healthy and free from defects: (Blind, Very thin, Sick,Limping severely )
    • Minimum age: Goat/sheep: 1 year old Cow: 2 years old

    4. Method of Slaughter
    • Intention (niyyah) for Qurban.
    • Very sharp knife used to minimize suffering
    • Animal laid on left side, facing Qiblah
    • Recite: Bismillahi Allahu Akbar. Sunnah to add:“Allahumma hadha minka wa laka, Allahumma taqabbal minni.”
    • Also say out very loudly so that the voice can be heard very clearly when recorded:
      • I slaughter this animal on behalf of ________________________ (name/s of qurban participant) this day, ____________ (say out the date of the slaughter) for the sake of Allah SWT.
    • Display a piece of paper that contains, very clearly, the name/s of all qurban participant/s for the particular animal being slaughtered.
    • Take some photographs and/or videos so that qurban participants can be assured that their qurban has been executed.
    • Cut the throat, windpipe, and jugular veins (at least 3 out of 4 major passages)

    6. Other Important Points
    • The slaughterer can be paid money to do the slaughtering, but he cannot be paid from the meat of the animal slaughtered. (However, he can be gifted the meat, just like other Muslims)
    • Knife must be very sharp and cannot be sharpened in front of the animal
    • Animal must be respected and treated very kindly
    • Do not slaughter one animal in front of another
    • Everybody (including women and children) can witness and learn

    Guide to Performing a Marriage Solemnization Ceremony (Akad Nikah)


    1. Pre-Ceremony Checks


    Make sure the following are complete before the ceremony begins:

    • Consent from both bride and groom
    • Presence of the Wali (usually the bride’s father or closest male guardian)
    • Two adult Muslim male witnesses
    • Agreed mahar (dowry) — amount and form
    • Legal documents completed (depending on local laws/registries)

    2. Begin the Ceremony


    a) Praise and Reminder (Khutbah Nikah): Start with khutbah nikah (you may use a short Arabic khutbah):
    "Innal hamdalillah, nahmaduhu wa nasta’inuhu wa nastaghfiruhu..."

    Briefly remind the couple:

    • About taqwa (God-consciousness)
    • About marriage as an act of ibadah
    • About mutual rights and responsibilities

    b) Declaration of Purpose: Explain that this is a solemn Islamic contract and both parties are entering it willingly and sincerely.


    3. Ijab and Qabul (Offer and Acceptance)


    The Wali (or wali hakim) says:
    “I marry you to my daughter, [name of bride], with a dowry of [Amount] peso, paid in cash.”

    The Groom responds clearly:
    “I accept the marriage of [name of bride] with that dowry, paid in cash.”

    Ensure clarity and fluency. There should be no hesitation or joking. It’s best to practice beforehand if necessary.


    4. Confirmation by Witnesses


    Ask both witnesses: “Is it valid?”
    They should both reply: “Yes, it is valid”


    5. Du'a & Closing


    Recite a short du’a for the couple:
    "Barakallahu laka wa baraka ‘alayka wa jama‘a baynakuma fi khayr."
    (Meaning - “May Allah bless you, and shower His blessings on you, and unite you both in goodness.”)

    Conclude with a reminder to the couple about being kind, patient, and upholding the rights of one another.
    Signing of documents should also be done where applicable.

    Brief Guide to Muslim Funeral Rites (Jenazah)

    🛌 1. When a Person Is Close to Death

    • Encourage the dying person to say the shahadah: "Laa ilaaha illallah" (There is no god but Allah)
    • Sit calmly and make du’a for ease and forgiveness.
    • If possible, position the person lying on their right side facing the Qiblah (or on their back with face turned toward Qiblah).
    • Do not wail or cry loudly.
    • Once the soul has departed, close the eyes, and tie the jaw (to keep it closed) and feet (to keep them together).

    🧼 2. Ghusl (Ritual Washing)

    • Done by close family or trusted Muslims of the same gender.
    • The body is washed an odd number of times (usually 3, 5, or more if needed) with clean water and scented soap or camphor for the final rinse.
    • Private parts must be covered at all times.
    • Begin washing from the right side, then the left.
    • After ghusl, dry the body and apply a little perfume (excluding the private parts).

    ⚰️ 3. Kafan (Shrouding)

    • Body is wrapped in white cloth (kafan) — preferably 3 layers for men, 5 for women.
    • Cloth should cover the whole body. Fasten with ties at the head, waist, and feet.

    🕌 4. Salat al-Janazah (Funeral Prayer)

    • Performed in congregation (at a mosque, prayer area, or cemetery).
    • It is a communal obligation (fard kifayah).
    • Performed while standing only — no ruku‘ or sujud.
    • Format: Takbir 1: Al-Fatihah, Takbir 2: Salawat on the Prophet ﷺ (e.g., Allahumma salli ‘ala Muhammad), Takbir 3: Du’a for the deceased, Takbir 4: Short concluding du’a and taslim (one or both sides),

    ⚰️ 5. Burial

    • The body is taken to the cemetery and placed into the grave on the right side facing the Qiblah.
    • A short du’a or reminder may be given at the graveside.
    • Fill the grave with soil, each person may place a handful.
    • Say: "Minhaa khalaqnaakum wa feehaa nu’eedukum wa minhaa nukhrijukum taaratan ukhraa"
      (“From it We created you, into it We shall return you, and from it We shall bring you out again.”)

    🤲 6. After Burial

    Make du’a for the deceased.It is sunnah to stay briefly at the grave and pray for the person’s steadfastness during questioning.

    APPENDIX 24 - ISLAMIC FAMILY COUNSELING

    - Brief Guide to Muslim-Based Family Counseling

    Islamic Family Counseling Guide

    1. Begin with the Right Intention (Niyyah)

    • Intend to counsel for the sake of Allah. Keep the matter confidential. Do not relay any of what’s discussed to others if there is no need to.
    • Make du’a for wisdom, patience, and sincerity.

    2. Create a Safe and Respectful Space

    • Ensure privacy and emotional safety.
    • Speak calmly, with rahmah (compassion) and adab (manners).
    • Encourage each party to speak — but no interrupting, mocking, or blaming.

    3. Clarify Roles and Goals

    • Ask each person: “What do you hope to achieve from this session?”
    • Define everyone’s role in the family (e.g., husband, wife, parent, child) in light of the Qur’an and Sunnah.

    4. Use Islamic Principles to Guide the Discussion

    Draw upon key values:

    • Taqwa: God-consciousness in words and actions
    • Shura (consultation): Involve each other in decision-making
    • Sabr (patience) & ‘Afw (forgiveness): Encourage letting go of grudges
    • Rights and responsibilities: Use teachings of the Prophet ﷺ as reference

    5. Address Core Issues, Not Just Symptoms

    • Explore the root causes (e.g., communication breakdown, unmet needs, stress)
    • Use reflective questions like:
      “What do you feel is missing?”
      “What has hurt you most?”
      “What could you do differently with Allah in mind?”

    6. Encourage Practical Steps

    • Suggest small, realistic actions (e.g., weekly family du’a time, one-on-one talks)
    • Recommend conflict-free spaces: salah together, Qur’an recitation, family meals
    • Assign tasks if needed (e.g., journaling, du’a lists, seeking forgiveness)

    🙌 7. End with Du’a and Reconnection

    • Recite a brief du’a together for healing and unity: -“Rabbana hab lana min azwajina wa dhurriyyatina qurrata a’yun, waj‘alna lil-muttaqina imama.” (Qur’an 25:74)
    • Encourage follow-up sessions if needed.
    Important Note:
    If there is abuse (physical, emotional, or verbal), neglect, talk of divorce or separation, or signs of mental illness or addiction, refer them to an ustaz, imam, or qualified counsellor who can help better.
