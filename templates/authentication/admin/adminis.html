Roles

The roles of the Municipality Islamic Center (MIC), Barangay Community Center (BCC), Masjid, and Musolla, are given in APPENDIX 1A, while the job specifications of the Regional Director (RD), Person-In-Charge (PIC), Center Secretary (SMIC), Center Treasurer (TMIC) Asatiz, Du’at, Vehicle Person-In-Charge (VO), Chief Recruiter (CR), etc, are given in APPENDIX 4.

Opening a New Center (MIC and/or BCC)

  • label_important Regional Directors (RD), Persons-In-Charge (PIC), or other individuals appointed by BBM, are required to identify potential new centers to be opened. Our aim is to have one Municipality Islamic Center (MIC) in every municipality in Cebu, and one Community Islamic Center (BCC) in every barangay in Cebu. There are about 1,200 barangays in Cebu, which should make us very busy in the next few years.
  • label_important Every MIC must aim to open no less than 2 BCCs every year.
  • label_important
    The criteria for a new BCC are:
    • nest_wifi_router the location has more than 20 reverts,
    • nest_wifi_router the cost of rent is reasonable,
    • nest_wifi_router it is reasonably easily accessible by road,
    • nest_wifi_router it is large enough to allow for prayers to be held for at least 20 people,
    • nest_wifi_router basic facilities such as toilets and ample living spaces are available and reasonably comfortable.
    • nest_wifi_router It is at least 2km away from the nearest BCC.
  • label_important Once BBM has approved of the place, permits and approvals from the authorities and regulatory bodies (SEC, DSWD, barangay, etc.) must be acquired. Related aspects such as to rent or buy, to renovate, or to build the center will be duly discussed. The BCC can be a rented house of not more than 5k pesos/month or it can be an ODFI-owned building.
  • label_important
    Every MIC must have the following office bearers:
    • nest_wifi_router one Person-In-Charge (PIC)
    • nest_wifi_router one Secretary (SMIC)
    • nest_wifi_router one Treasurer (TMIC).
  • label_important The 3 office bearers of the MIC (PIC, SMIC, and TMIC) cannot be the same person or person(s) related by blood to each other or is/are each other’s spouse. There should also be at least one ustaz or ustazah, and at least 1 da’ee, and a number of trainees.
  • label_important Every MIC must have at least one van/ jeepney/ multicab and 2 motorbikes for transport and at least one staff must have a valid licence to drive.
  • label_important Every BCC must have at least one imam who is local. He is the caretaker of the BCC. He should already be living in the community not far from where the BCC is located. An operation budget of not more than 10,000 peso/month will be granted to the BCC, but the money is NOT to pay the imam. Rather, it is to be used for rental or to buy refreshments whenever there are classes held there, or to pay for utility bills, or other job-related expenses of the BCC. The imam does not get any salary as this is a voluntary position that he does just to attain Allah’s pleasure. The imam maintains his regular salaried job or whatever livelihood means he already has, and does his imam duties outside of his salaried/ current livelihood working hours.
  • label_important Every PIC should identify at least 3 local people to become imam at every BCC, and they should already have means of livelihood. All imams are encouraged to upgrade their knowledge of Islam continuously, and if they show potential, may be sent for more Islamic education that may lead to them becoming asatiz.
  • label_important There should be at least one motorbike stationed at every BCC.
