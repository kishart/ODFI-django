Who are We?

We are a charity-based NGO based in Cebu, Philippines. We have been incorporated under the Securities Exchange Commission with a paid-up capital of 1 million pesos. ODFI focuses on managing the financial and legal aspects of the Dakwah Bil Haal Project in the Philippines. All Islamic centers (Municipality Islamic Centers, MIC) and their satellites in the barangays (Barangay Islamic Community Centers, BCC) are considered as chapters under ODFI.

The founding Board of Trustees (BOT) comprise 3 Filipinos and 2 Malaysians: Dato’ Abdul Razak Kechik, Heidie Lampera Aplece (Treasurer), Rozita Mohd Fajar Ali, Sofronia Olila Ebale (Secretary), and Bonifacio Quino Uy Jr. (President). At the time of writing (June, 2025), there is a BOARD OF TRUSTEE that serves ODFI, comprising Dato' Abdul Razak Kechik, Abdul Rahman Uy, Sofronia Ebale, and Heidie Aplece. We are in the process to include Salim Danny Daham, Raian Estrera Tampus, and John Daler Tejones Mancao as additional Board of Trustees members.

A Brief History of ODFI

2014

Malaysia Relief Agency (MRA) visited Cebu in its humanitarian quest following Hurricane Haiyan.

2016

Briged Bakti Malaysia (BBM) took over from MRA under its International Outreach Programs, led by Dato' Dr Abdul Razak, in collaboration with Islamic Outreach ABIM Kelantan (IOAK) and MRA.

2022

Over 200,000 Cebuanos and more than 200 people from Negros have embraced Islam as part of the outreach mission.

April 2025

Nearly 80 Municipality Islamic Centers (MICs), Barangay Community Centers (BCC), and masjids established in 23 municipalities and cities.

January 2020

One Daawah Foundation (ODFI) was officially incorporated with SEC approval to manage all financial and legal aspects of ODFI programs.

ODFI Overview

ODFI Objectives

  • Finance and facilitate the establishment of MICs, BCCs, and SCCs for religious education and services.
  • Facilitate halal-based economic empowerment programs for the poor.
  • Lease/acquire land for halal activities and allocate space for a Muslim cemetery.
  • Provide humanitarian aid to disaster-affected communities.
  • Cooperate with government agencies like NCMF on community issues.

ODFI Strategies

  • Establish strategic partnerships for support and expertise.
  • Train and empower du’at, asatiz, and community leaders.
  • Promote Islamic education and support services at all levels.
  • Encourage community-level economic activities.
  • Engage in sustainable economic ventures.
  • Build masjids and community centers.
  • Create an asset bank for future use.
  • Maintain comprehensive records of all operations and assets.

ODFI Basic Financial Principles

  • Operate based on amanah, efficiency, frugality, and non-wastefulness.
  • All financial transactions must be halal.
  • Accepts donations, grants, zakat, sadaqah, and halal profits.
  • Maintain transparent and accurate financial records.
  • Spending must align with community benefits and mission.
  • OPEX spending must be BBM-approved to be reimbursed.
  • Monthly reports are required from centers to BBM.
  • BBM audits financial records annually.
  • Financial falsification results in disciplinary action.
  • Strictly no cronyism or nepotism.

ODFI Basic Operational Principles

  • Programs are needs-based and community-involved.
  • Respect for all individuals, regardless of background.
  • Centers must uphold safety, dignity, and cleanliness.

ODFI Basic Human Resource (HR) Principles

  • HR is rooted in amanah, integrity, and professionalism.
  • Islamic and respectful working environment encouraged.
  • Selection is merit-based; no cronyism or nepotism allowed.
  • Workforce must adhere to Islamic manners and teamwork.
  • Conflicts should be resolved calmly and privately under Islamic values.

ODFI Workforce Training & Development Principles

  • Training is based on amanah, integrity, and effectiveness.
  • Workforce is expected to master Islamic topics and continue learning.
  • Learning should be applied in daily personal and professional life.
